Conference Paper (1st Author)

Bangla Diarizz: Domain-Adapted Speaker Diarization for Bengali Long-Form Audio — I am the 1st author.

Read the full paper on ResearchGate

This work addresses a key low-resource speech challenge: speaker diarization for Bengali ("who spoke when"). The system was developed for the DL Sprint 4.0 Bengali Speaker Diarization competition (BUET CSE Fest 2026) and focuses on targeted domain adaptation over language-agnostic baselines.

Core contributions

  • Surgically replaced both segmentation and embedding modules of the pretrained Pyannote-Community-1 pipeline.
  • Fine-tuned the segmentation model on the competition dataset using the diarizers library.
  • Replaced speaker embeddings with WeSpeaker ResNet34-LM.
  • Introduced a post-processing stitching mechanism to merge short intra-speaker gaps and reduce fragmentation.

Results & impact

  • Achieved competitive Diarization Error Rate (DER) on the Bengali-Loop benchmark.
  • Reduced inference time from 1h 22m to approximately 36m across 14 test audio files.
  • Delivered a 56% wall-clock speed improvement.
